# Theme Taxonomy — v1

*Last updated: June 25, 2026*

Maximum 3 themes per beat. Tag for what is present, not what is implied.

---

## Core themes

These are expected to recur throughout the corpus and will anchor the main clusters in the galaxy view.

| tag | definition |
|-----|------------|
| `#tool-as-mirror` | AI reflects something back about the narrator's thinking, communication, or identity |
| `#limit-of-the-tool` | Encountering a capability ceiling in what AI can do |
| `#limit-of-the-self` | Realising the tool can only go as far as the narrator's own knowledge |
| `#dependency` | Noticing a reliance on AI forming — consciously or not |
| `#delegation` | Consciously and successfully handing something to the machine |
| `#reframe` | A frustration or failure converted into a principle or new understanding |
| `#observation-of-others` | Watching how people around the narrator relate to AI |
| `#language-and-expression` | Moments where how the narrator speaks or writes becomes the subject |

---

## Emergent themes

Present in the corpus but may not persist. Either disappear or grow into core themes over time.

| tag | definition |
|-----|------------|
| `#builder-identity` | Questioning what it means to ship and build as a designer using AI |
| `#ai-policy-friction` | Institutional constraints on AI adoption — rules, permissions, enterprise limits |
| `#mobile-and-mobility` | AI use outside desk context — commute, walking, outdoors |
| `#bilingual-cognition` | French/English interference as a signal layer in expression and thinking |
| `#build-surface-constraints` | The specific frustration of the gap between using Claude and building with it |

---

**Tagging rules**
- `#delegation` applies only to conscious, successful handoff — not failed attempts
- `#limit-of-the-tool` and `#limit-of-the-self` often co-occur but are distinct — apply both only when both are genuinely present
- `#tool-as-mirror` requires the reflection to come *through* the AI interaction, not merely alongside it
